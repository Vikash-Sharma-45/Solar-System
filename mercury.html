Mercury: The Swiftest Planet

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in our solar system, known for its extreme temperatures and lack of atmosphere. It is a rocky planet with a surface marked by craters, similar to Earth's Moon.

Key Characteristics

  • Size and Composition: Diameter of about 4,880 kilometers (3,032 miles), primarily composed of iron and silicate rock.
  • Orbit and Rotation: Orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 57.91 million kilometers (36 million miles) with an orbit period of 88 Earth days.
  • Temperature Extremes: Daytime temperatures can reach up to 430 째C (800 째F), while nighttime can drop to -180 째C (-290 째F).
  • Surface Features: Heavily cratered surface with notable features like the Caloris Basin.
  • Atmosphere: Very thin atmosphere composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium.
  • Exploration: First visited by Mariner 10; more recently studied by the MESSENGER spacecraft.

Mercury offers valuable insights into the formation of the solar system and continues to be a subject of scientific interest and exploration.
