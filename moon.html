The Moon: Earth's Natural Satellite

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest moon in the solar system. It has been a source of fascination for humanity throughout history, influencing culture, mythology, and scientific exploration.

Key Characteristics

  • Size and Composition: Diameter of approximately 3,474 kilometers (2,159 miles), about one-quarter the size of Earth.
  • Orbit and Rotation: Orbits Earth at an average distance of about 384,400 kilometers (238,855 miles) and takes 27.3 days to complete one orbit and rotation.
  • Phases and Eclipses: Goes through eight distinct phases each lunar month and can experience lunar and solar eclipses.
  • Surface Features: Marked by craters, mountains, and dark plains known as "maria," formed by ancient volcanic activity.
  • Gravity and Atmosphere: Has a gravitational pull about one-sixth that of Earth and a very thin atmosphere called an exosphere.

The Moon remains an essential object of study in astronomy and planetary science, with ongoing exploration efforts aimed at returning humans to its surface.
